Safety Tips Page for CovidTracker

A list of important safety tips based on CDC's and WHO's Safety Tips

Wash Hands often

Wash hands with soap regularly for atleast 20 minutes. Otherwise, use Hand Sanitizers with atleast 60% alcohol

Face Coverings

It is recommended (and required in some places) to use a face coverin in public to prevent yourself from getting sick or to prevent yourself from passing the virus

Monitor your health

Always be alert for any COVID-19 symptoms and check temperature if symptoms are present. Get tested if you feel like you have COVID-19

Clean and Disinfect

Clean and Disinfect touched surfaces daily. This includes tables, doorknobs, light switches, countertops, handles, desks, phones, keyboards, toilets, faucets, and sinks.

Cover Coughs and Sneezes

If you are around others and do not have on your cloth face covering, remember to always cover your mouth and nose with a tissue when you cough or sneeze or use the inside of your elbow and do not spit

Avoid Close Contact

Avoid contact with people who are sick. If possible, maintain atleast a 6ft(~2m) distance between people in public

Common COVID-19 Myths [Based on WHO]

Thermal Scanners/Thermometers can detect COVID-19

Thermal scanners are effective in detecting people who have a fever (i.e. have a higher than normal body temperature). They cannot detect people who are infected with COVID-19. There are many causes of fever.

A lot of COVID_19 infections are found to be asymptomatic and cannot be detected by Thermal Scanners. Also, people who have fever can have them through multiple different causes, and having a fever is not definitive proof of COVID-19

Prolonged Use of Masks cause CO2 Intoxication

The prolonged use of medical masks can be uncomfortable. However, it does not lead to CO2 intoxication nor oxygen deficiency. When placed correctly, they will be completely safe for a long period of time and will not cause CO2 intoxication

While wearing a medical mask, make sure it fits properly and that it is tight enough to allow you to breathe normally. Do not re-use a disposable mask and always change it as soon as it gets damp.

5G spreads COVID-19

Viruses cannot travel on radio waves/mobile networks. COVID-19 is spreading in many countries that do not have 5G mobile networks. Currently, it is widely accepted that 5G cannot spread COVID-19 and is not the cause of the virus.

COVID-19 is spread through respiratory droplets when an infected person coughs, sneezes or speaks. People can also be infected by touching a contaminated surface and then their eyes, mouth or nose.
